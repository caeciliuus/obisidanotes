Force

  • What is a force?
    • Causes a change in motionmotion
    • At its simplest level, forces are push/pulls applied to an object
  • Forces are interactions; there are no true external forces, only pairs of forces
  • Dynamics: study of why motion happens or changes
    • Kinematics studies the acceleration of the body, dynamics asks the question "is the body moving?"
    • Objects can change velocity thru direction or acceleration change
  • Balanced force: no change in motion; unbalanced force: change in motion [acceleration]
  • Force is a vector, with Newtons used as units (N); = net force
  • Mass is a measure of the amount of matter contained within an object ⇒ also a measure of inertia as it provides resistance to motion change
    • Metric unit: g
    • SI unit: kg
  • The center of mass is the point associated with an object around which all mass is balanced
    • Tower of Pisa almost fell when its center of gravity was dangerously close to leaving its support base
  • A system is an array of objects that interact; an object is just a point particle
    • You define the system, and once you do so, don't change it
  • Free body diagram: reduces object to a dot and shows all forces acting on it

Potential energyPotential energy


Kinetic energy is the energy an object has by virtue of its motion. Potential energy is independent of motion; it arises from the object's position (or the system's configuration). For example, a ball at the edge of a tabletop has energy that could be transformed into kinetic energy if it falls from the table. An arrow in an archer's pulled-back bow has energy that could be trans-formed into kinetic energy if the archer releases the arrow. Both of these are examples of potential energy, the energy an object or system has by virtue of its position or configuration. In each case, work was done on the object to put it in the given configuration (the ball was lifted to the tabletop, the bowstring was pulled back), and since work is the means of transferring energy, these things have stored energy that can be converted into kinetic energy. This is potential energy, denoted by .

Because there are different types of forces, there are different types of potential energy. The ball at the edge of the tabletop provides an example of gravitational potential energy, , which is the energy stored by virtue of an object's position in a gravitational field.
